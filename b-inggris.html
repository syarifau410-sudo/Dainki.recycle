Plastic Waste

Waste is the residual waste from a human production process that has no primary value or function and can negatively impact the environment if not managed properly. Waste is divided into three categories based on its form, its constituent compounds, and its source. The impact of waste is that it can cause soil, water, and air pollution. Waste can be solid, liquid, or gaseous and can be categorized as organic or inorganic waste.

Plastic waste is used or unused plastic material that is no longer valuable or useful. Plastic waste is difficult to decompose and takes a long time, up to 500 years. Sources of plastic waste include single-use product packaging and durable products.

The impacts of plastic waste include harm to wildlife, ecosystem damage, impacts on human health, and contributions to the climate crisis. Plastic waste is considered inorganic waste because it does not decompose naturally. If disposed of carelessly, inorganic waste can cause soil and water pollution.

Plastic waste must be reduced to reduce the impact of pollution. There are many ways to reduce plastic waste. One of them is by recycling. Our group is recycling plastic into Eco bricks, we use them to make table legs.
